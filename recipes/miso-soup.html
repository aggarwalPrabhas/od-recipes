Comfort in a Bowl: Miso Soup

Miso soup is a traditional Japanese dish made with a savory broth called dashi and miso paste, a fermented soybean seasoning. It’s a staple in Japanese cuisine, often served as a side dish with rice or meals.

The soup typically includes soft tofu cubes, seaweed (wakame), and green onions. Light yet flavorful, miso soup is not only comforting but also rich in probiotics and nutrients, making it both delicious and nourishing.

Miso Soup Ingredients

  • Dashi (Japanese soup stock – made from kombu and bonito flakes)
  • Miso paste (white or red)
  • Tofu (soft or silken, cubed)
  • Wakame (dried seaweed)
  • Green onions (thinly sliced)

Steps to Make Miso Soup

  1. Prepare the Dashi Heat water and add kombu (dried kelp). Just before boiling, remove the kombu and add bonito flakes. Simmer briefly, then strain the liquid to get clear dashi.
  2. Add Ingredients Return the dashi to the pot. Add wakame and cubed tofu, and let it simmer for a few minutes.
  3. Mix in Miso Paste Turn off the heat. Dissolve miso paste in a ladle with a bit of hot broth, then stir it back into the soup. Avoid boiling after adding miso to preserve flavor and nutrients.
  4. Garnish and Serve Add chopped green onions. Serve hot and enjoy!
