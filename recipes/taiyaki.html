Taiyaki (Japanese Fish-Shaped Cake Waffle)

Taiyaki is a fish-shaped Japanese pastry made from a pancake-like batter. It is cooked in a special mold that gives it a crispy outside and a soft, fluffy inside.

Traditionally filled with sweet red bean paste, Taiyaki also comes with other fillings like custard, chocolate, or cheese. It’s a beloved street food enjoyed warm at festivals and markets across Japan.

Taiyaki Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • Baking powder
  • Sugar
  • Eggs
  • Milk or water
  • Sweet red bean paste (anko) — traditional filling
  • Optional fillings: custard, chocolate, cheese

How to Make Taiyaki

  1. Prepare the batter: Mix flour, baking powder, sugar, eggs, and milk (or water) until smooth.
  2. Heat the Taiyaki mold: Preheat the fish-shaped mold and lightly grease it.
  3. Pour batter: Pour some batter into one side of the mold.
  4. Add filling: Place a spoonful of sweet red bean paste (or your choice of filling) in the center.
  5. Cover with batter: Pour more batter on top to cover the filling.
  6. Cook: Close the mold and cook for a few minutes on each side until golden brown and crispy.
  7. Remove and cool slightly: Carefully take out the Taiyaki and let it cool a bit before eating.
