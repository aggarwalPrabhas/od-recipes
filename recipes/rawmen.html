Ramen

The Art of Japanese Ramen

Ramen is a beloved Japanese noodle dish known for its rich broth, chewy wheat noodles, and flavorful toppings. Its origins trace back to China, but it has become a staple of Japanese cuisine with many regional variations. Common broth types include soy sauce (shoyu), miso, salt (shio), and pork bone (tonkotsu), each offering a unique taste experience.

Typical toppings include sliced pork (chashu), marinated soft-boiled eggs, bamboo shoots, green onions, and seaweed. Whether enjoyed in a small ramen shop or made at home, ramen combines comfort and depth of flavor in every bowl.

Ingredients

  • Broth: Pork bones, garlic, ginger, onion, soy sauce, mirin, salt
  • Noodles: Fresh or dried ramen noodles
  • Toppings: Sliced pork (chashu), soft-boiled egg, bamboo shoots, nori, green onions
